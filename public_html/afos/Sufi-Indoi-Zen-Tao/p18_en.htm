A woman undertook the care of a monk. She made a shack at the edge of the woods; she fed and clothed him.
Twenty years passed and then she thought: "Let's see if my efforts were worthwhile or in vain." So she sent a beautiful young girl, hungry for love, and she told her how to behave. The young girl went to the monk, she approached him and confessed that she was burning with passion and asked him to cool her down with his love. He replied: "I'm a dead man". The girl leaned towards him, took his hand and started to caress it. "I'm like a piece of dry wood", the monk repeated. The girl gave up trying and returned to the woman.
"He should have shown concern for your situation" the woman said. "He should have shown understanding towards your torment. Zen isn't morals; it is infinite understanding. So, were my efforts of twenty years wasted? She then went and set fire to the monk's shack. And didn't even ask about him again.
